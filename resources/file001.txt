article 1: how does a search engine work? a search engine is a complex system of programs and algorithms designed to search for information on the internet or within corporate systems. the primary stages of a search engine's operation include indexing, ranking, and result retrieval. initially, the engine crawls pages or files to create an index, which is a catalog of keywords and documents. upon receiving a search query, it finds relevant documents and ranks them according to their relevance to the query. relevance is determined using specific formulas that consider the frequency and placement of words. the result of the search engine's work is a list of the most relevant results for the user.
